how it's played

 In this version of nim the  game board consists of four rows of increasing amounts of tokens: 1,3,5 and 7.
On each players turn he or she can take as many tokens as they wish from the game board, but those tokens must all come from the same row.
In other words you can only take tokens from a single row on each turn. Play continues until there is only one token left on the game board.
The players whose turn it is when one token remains is the loser.

the secret of nim

Nim can always be won if you know the secret  and strategy for winning.  There is an easy trick for mastering the version of nim presented in
this app. To start, think of all the tokens on the game board as existing in groups of one, two and four.

    |            1 = 1
   |||           1+2 =3
  |||||          4 +1 = 5
 |||||||         4 +2 +1 = 7

When the game starts the game board is balanced. This means that there are an even number of groups of ones, twos and fours on the board.  
When a token is removed from the board the tree becomes unbalanced. If you can rebalance the tree on your turn, leaving an even number
of the groups, you can guarantee a win. As you might have already guessed, it is an advantage to not have the first turn.

As an example lets say we have two players Mark and Jane. Mark goes first removing 3 tokens from the 3rd row. The board now look like

     |            1 = 1
   |||           1+2 =3
  ||             2 = 2
 |||||||         4 +2 +1 = 7

Marks move effectively destroyed a group of four and created a new group of two. On Jane's turn she should remove a group of four, two and one to rebalance the tree. There is only one row where this is possible, row four.   Jane  decides to remove tokens from the fourth row. the board now looks like

    |            1 = 1
   |||           1+2 =3
  ||             2 = 2

As you can see the tree is balanced again having an even number of groups of twos and ones. This means on marks turn he can only leave an unbalanced game board and give Jane the advantage.  Look at the game board as it stand now and imagine all the moves Mark might make. There is no scenario in which he can win unless Jane makes a mistake. This is the secret of Nim, leaving a balanced game board at the end of your turn so that you are always at an advantage to execute the winning move!

Practice playing nim in the apps player vs. ai setting to hone your skills. Take care to analyze the game board each turn and leave it balanced. As an added challenge try letting the ai player go second. Can you catch the ai making a mistake?
